Gross Domestic Product
  • Definition
  • Gross domestic product (GDP) is the monetary value of all the finished goods and services produced within a country's borders in a specific time period.
  • What does GDP signify?
  • For example, if the year-to-year GDP is up 3%, this is thought to mean that the economy has grown by 3% over the last year.
  • How is GDP calculated?
  • The calculation can be done in one of two ways: either by adding up what everyone earned in a year (income approach), or by adding up what everyone spent (expenditure method). Logically, both measures should arrive at roughly the same total.
  • How does GDP differ from GNP?
  • GNP(Gross National Product) differs from GDP in that GNP measures the productivity of a nation’s citizens regardless of their locales, as opposed to the GDP’s measurement of production by geographic location.
